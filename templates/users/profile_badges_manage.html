About Badges

The School of Webcraft assessments and badges are part of a pilot effort to give you a way to recognize the learning that occurs at SoW, demonstrate your skills, build reputation in the community and eventually, get jobs. The current badges displayed were earned during the initial phase of the pilot in the January-April cycle of SoW courses. Each badge links back to the work that was submitted and the endorsements/feedback from the assessment and this information will continue to be linked to from your badge wherever you decide to share it, adding more validity and evidence to your skills and achievements.

While these are alpha badges, they are endorsed by Mozilla and will continue to be credentials that you can use to share with various stakeholders like potential employers or peer communities. In order to allow you to share your badges with other sites and communities, P2PU is has plugged into the Mozilla Open Badge Infrastructure (OBI), which allows you to manage a single badge collection, including badges from SoW as well as other badge issuers and learning providers across the web, and then manage those badges and share them out accordingly. To take advantage of this, you need to push your badges to the OBI.

What Will Happen When I Click Send

The OBI will ask you for an email address - this will be your unique identifier to combine and manage your badges. Mozilla is using BrowserID, which will allow you to maintain a single identity through your email address without having to worry about passwords, etc. For more on Verified Email, see: https://browserid.org/ Once you have provided an email address, the OBI will ask you to accept P2PU (SoW) as an issuer of badges (essentially saying its okay for P2PU to push badges you have earned on their site into your central collection). Once you accept, your badges will be pushed into the OBI and will exist at alpha.badge.org for you to access the badges, manage them and share them out.

Alpha version

The OBI is currently in the alpha version with means limited functionality and only 1 issuer - P2PU. The Backpack (management interface) functionality is limited but will expand considerably with beta. Mozilla is launching the beta version which will be full functionality and a handful of additional issuers in early September. Then the public 1.0 launch will happen in early January 2012 and at that point, all issuers can join and there will be even more of a badge ecosystem. If you send your badges now, they will be rolled over with each release and you will seamlessly capitalize on the new features as they are completed.
